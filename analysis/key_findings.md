# Key Findings - Water Quality Analysis 2022

## Executive Summary

This analysis evaluated 300 water samples across 5 regions and 3 source types, assessing compliance with WHO safety standards for pH, nitrate, and lead parameters.

---

##  Critical Findings

### 1. Overall Water Quality Crisis

> **73.3% of water samples are contaminated** - exceeding safe limits for at least one parameter.

- Only **26.7%** of samples meet all safety standards

### 2. Lead Contamination - Primary Concern

> **Lead exceedance rate: 42.7%** - the highest among all parameters

**Implications:**
- Lead exposure causes neurological damage, especially in children
- Possible corrosion in distribution pipes
- Requires immediate infrastructure assessment

### 3. Regional Hotspots

| Region | Contamination Rate | Severity |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| South Zone | 76.4% | Highest |
| North Zone | 76.2% | Highest |
| Central Zone | 74.2% | High |
| East Zone | 70.6% | Moderate |
| West Zone | 69.6% | Moderate |

### 4. Tap Water Risk

> **Tap water shows 77.8% contamination rate** - highest among all sources

### 5. Seasonal Nitrate Patterns

**Peak Nitrate Months:**
- **February**: 47.15 mg/L (highest)
- **October**: 42.91 mg/L

##  Actionable Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (0-3 months)

1. **Lead Testing Program** - Test all tap water sources for lead
2. **North and South Zone Priority** - Deploy mobile testing units

### Short-term Actions (3-6 months)

1. **Seasonal Monitoring** - Enhanced nitrate testing during spring/autumn
2. **Source Protection** - Agricultural runoff management

### Long-term Actions (6-12 months)

1. **Infrastructure Improvement** - Water treatment plant upgrades
2. **Policy Development** - Stricter water quality standards
